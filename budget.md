# Budget

Total request: $8,800

## API and model access: $2,000

Model access for repeated benchmark runs, paired high-capacity and low-capacity prompts, robustness checks, and reruns after rubric revisions.

## Scenario construction and dataset work: $2,250

Support for building the paired CrisisBench dataset, including scenario writing, metadata, capacity-axis tagging, and failure-mode tagging.

## Human scoring and annotation: $2,000

Manual scoring of model responses against the rubric. The project will prioritize human scoring because the benchmark tests subtle failures such as capacity blindness, false feasibility, authority hallucination, and unsafe delegation.

## Expert review: $1,750

Limited review of scenarios and scoring criteria by people with relevant domain knowledge. This is meant to catch unrealistic scenarios, weak assumptions, and scoring mistakes.

## Benchmark documentation and public release: $300

Minimal public documentation, repository setup, dataset cards, and benchmark instructions using low-cost public infrastructure.

## Paper preparation and dissemination: $250

Basic formatting and publication-related costs. The paper writing itself will be done by the project lead.

## Reserve for reruns or annotation checks: $250

Small reserve for additional model runs, reruns, or scoring checks after the first pass.
